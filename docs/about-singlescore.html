About Single Score Conversion

How characterisation results are converted to single scores (Pt) for each input.

Method

Approach

From characterisation to single score

Single scores are computed by multiplying each characterisation midpoint result by the EF 3.0 weighting factor Fₖ = WF / NF (from singlescore - singlescore_conversion.csv) and summing across categories. Units are Pt (points).

Data sources

Inputs used

  • Characterisation: pivot_app/data/characterisation.json
  • Weighting/normalisation: singlescore/singlescore - singlescore_conversion.csv
  • Outputs: pivot_app/data/singlescore.json and per-product files in singlescore/

Aggregation

Pesticide group sums

Herbicide, Insecticide, and Fungicide are provided as aggregates (plant + emissions), replacing the individual component records for clarity.

Units

What you see

All single-score results are expressed in Pt (points). Extremely small or large values may display in scientific notation in the UI for readability.

Methods

Environmental Footprint (EF 3.0)

Normalisation and weighting. This application uses the official EF 3.0 normalisation (NFₖ) and weighting (WFₖ) sets from the European Commission JRC. Global normalisation factors are from Sala et al. (2017), expressing per-capita burdens; weighting factors are from Sala et al. (2018), combining survey, expert, and robustness inputs.

Single-score formula. For each impact category k with characterisation result Cₖ, the EF single score is:

SS (Pt) = Σₖ (Cₖ / NFₖ × WFₖ)

The resulting unit is Pt (Points), allowing comparison across categories and inputs.
